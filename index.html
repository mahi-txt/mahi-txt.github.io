About 7 living people I saw while in said city for 45 minutes. 5 of which were also American university students. Why are we here? 2 of which were locking doors to their respective art gallery and convenience store. Quietest place I've experienced in a very long time. Standing between a cathedral and a pharmacy. Silent, I am the loudest thing around. I am a headless chicken. I spent an hour returning to the airport hotel room in which a monkey hanging from a rope awaited me. A light fixture. beauty, essex, & an apology for party rocking!!
Posted on 2024-05-31 at 8:23 AM

Some moments feel transitory and sometimes I ascribe their beauty to the fact that they must end. Suffering, though, isn’t defined by my belief in its end. I give it power to consume all, to give me a silent permission to abdicate responsibility, rhyme, and reason. I give it more of my space and time in hopes that if we let it grow, it will go away. I let it grow but I am no longer shocked, moved, or surprised by it. It happens and it’s a piece of me.
I suffer, I give it time, I heal for the rest of my life. I love, I run away from it, I regret it for the rest of my life. I look back and think it was beautiful and will I find it again?
Suffering is the tree that keeps growing right under me and beauty is the fleeting bird I am raised closer to. I carry the hope that we belong to the same branch.

monolithic beauty
Posted on 2024-06-27 at 2:49 PM

writing at work. in new york (not monroe, louisiana, for those of you i complained to)
the power of this city is not lost on me.
i spent the early years of my life here, not understanding who i was or where i was
i don’t know if i do now, but it feels different
less like being lost, more like being small in a place that understands smallness is not a weakness.


cultural monolith?
what does that mean and why can’t i get it out of my head?
monolith implies unwavering-ness. unpenetrability. indivisible and uniform
but the city feels like all of that and the opposite. fractured and loud and endlessly reinventing itself,
still somehow, undeniably one thing.
maybe that’s the monolith. not in the cold, stone way. more like something ancient and alive
maybe that’s what all the sitcoms were trying to bottle
this place has a mythology. a gravitational pull.
it’s bizarre to be here and not be fictional
i walk past buildings and remember shows i watch when i feel alone
i walk into restaurants that feel like movie sets
sometimes it’s like i’m trespassing inside someone else’s dream
sometimes it’s like i’ve finally been let into mine.


what made it so expensive here?
i’m young and texan and removed but nostalgic for an era i never really lived through
when a slice was a dollar and if you had anything to do between the hours of 8 and 6,
you were free to just be after that
dinner, drinks, hobbies, a cool apartment with fun trinkets because that was who you are.
not because you abandoned everyone and everything and yourself


i’m disillusioned
i’m used to paying the same for a drink as a pizza and a t-shirt (twenty dollars)
i’m used to feeling unlovable, like being loudly in love with someone is an act of trespass
and in a city that preaches unapologetic truth and being,
being loud about that in a place where your weight and being is welcome
because a monolith will not break,
a cultural monolith is not threatened by your propensities or your self-importance.
if it’s not a way to explore love, from any angle, it’s noise.


i’m a small monolith. i’m overwhelmed by my capacity to love and to be loved.
